RE: Software

I started working in the computer science world once I went to college. I didn't have a lot in mind for what I wanted to do, but I had imagined being an astronaut. Since reading Carl Sagan's Cosmos, space was an interest of mine. NASA doesn't seem like my place anymore, but I have found joy in the sense of creation that programming brings with it.

I chose web design specifically because it allows me to use visual and artistic reasoning (frontend) in combination with logical programming (backend). Basically, it's a fun time all around. In addition to web development, I spent my time at Madison sticking my thumbs into a lot of other pies. Graphics, AI, low-level architecture, and operating systems were all on the menu.

I would be more than glad to take a position working on any one of those things. I train fast.

RE: Art

In addition to software design, I also make art. Sometimes. When I get a free hour here or there.

This started a bit before college. I'm not sure exactly what started it. I do remember that I wanted to get a watercolor set. I think I had seen art sold by vendors in Italy and wanted to make something like it. It was simple, but it had captured so much nonetheless. The next Christmas, when I had a little spending money, I took a trip to the art store.

Since then I've received a Certificate in Studio Art from Madison, and I've even sold a couple pieces. I've experimented with watercolor, acrylic, oil, relief printmaking, charcoal drawing, comic art, digital art, and more.
